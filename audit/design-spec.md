# Candidate Experience Design Specification

## Strategic argument

PNC does not need more isolated prompt experiments. It needs a repeatable operating mechanism that lets process owners and engineers define, prove, operate, and reuse AI-assisted work without losing security, authority, or accountability.

## Hero contract

- Visible PNC identity above the fold.
- One dominant thesis: “Scale the work. Not just the prompt.”
- One dominant visual: six control clauses closing around AI-assisted work.
- One primary action advancing to the first-90-day plan.
- Scenario controls stay below the hero.

## Interaction contract

A useful illustrative baseline is loaded immediately. Four workflow scenarios change all six clauses, the release posture, the decision, rationale, measures, active controls, ARIA state, and assistive announcement as one transaction. Keyboard arrows, Home, End, click/touch, reset, rapid selection, and reduced motion are supported.

## Motion contract

The hero runs one short assembly and settles. It does not loop. Scenario changes produce bounded card-state transitions and settle. Reduced motion displays the complete end state immediately.

## Responsive contract

Desktop uses the full envelope scene and two-column studio. Tablet stacks the studio beneath controls. Mobile presents the envelope as a compact semantic illustration and the control clauses as readable records. No horizontal scroll is required at 390 or 320 pixels.

## Candidate positioning

Lead with direct operating and workflow work. Preserve titles and claim strength. Do not publish a rejection memo. Treat deep language/platform tenure and bank-internal knowledge as interview calibration boundaries, not the campaign’s public thesis.
