The operating pressure

The bank does not need more isolated AI moments.

It needs a repeatable way to translate process-owner intent into engineering requirements, preserve risk and decision boundaries, prove the workflow, and decide what should scale across internal functions.

01

Speed vs. control

Productivity gains matter, but data exposure, unsupported answers, and uncontrolled actions are unacceptable.

02

Experiment vs. engineer

A prompt becomes enterprise capability only when it is versioned, tested, observable, and maintainable.

03

Reuse vs. specificity

Common patterns create leverage; each process still carries distinct context, exceptions, and owners.

04

Automation vs. authority

AI can prepare, retrieve, classify, and route. People must retain consequential judgment and exception authority.

05

Capability vs. adoption

A workflow that tests well but is not trusted, used, or improved does not create operating value.

Decision architecture

What becomes easier to decide.

The control envelope is useful only if it improves real choices—not if it becomes documentation ceremony.

Scale

What is reusable?

Separate a durable prompt, retrieval, tool, evaluation, or operating pattern from the process-specific context around it.

Retain

What stays human?

Make consequential judgment, exception handling, approval, and accountability explicit before automation expands.

Stop

What should not proceed?

Hold work when the outcome is vague, sources are uncontrolled, permissions are excessive, or proof and recovery are inadequate.

First 90 days

Enter through the work, then earn the pattern.

The plan begins with process owners and engineering reality—not a preselected solution or invented view of internal maturity.

Days 1–30

Read the work

  • Map active demand, process owners, engineering interfaces, controls, and approval paths.
  • Select one bounded workflow with a visible baseline and accountable owner.
  • Define outcome, data class, human authority, evaluation set, and operating measures.
Days 31–60

Prove the envelope

  • Build and test the prompt/agent workflow as modular, versioned components.
  • Exercise normal, edge, refusal, injection, permissions, fallback, and exception cases.
  • Run a controlled pilot and review corrections, adoption, overrides, and process friction.
Days 61–90

Earn reuse

  • Separate process-specific context from reusable prompt, retrieval, tool, evaluation, and telemetry patterns.
  • Publish a practical review mechanism and named ownership model.
  • Recommend scale, repair, hold, or stop based on evidence—not enthusiasm.
