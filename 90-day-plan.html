Operating premise

Enter through one bounded workflow. Earn the reusable pattern.

This plan is intentionally hypothesis-driven. It does not presume access to PNC’s internal architecture, control procedures, process portfolio, or delivery cadence. The first month is designed to discover those facts with process owners and engineering partners.
DAYS 1–15

Orient to the work and decision system

  • Meet the hiring manager, engineering peers, process owners, security, data, model-risk/responsible-AI, quality, platform, and adoption partners.
  • Map demand intake, process selection, requirements, data access, design, evaluation, approval, release, monitoring, and change ownership.
  • Understand the intended unit of scale and the role’s decision rights.
DAYS 16–30

Select a bounded proof workflow

  • Choose a process with an accountable owner, visible baseline, manageable data boundary, representative users, and reversible scope.
  • Document the AI Work Control Envelope: intent, context, authority, evaluation, human decision, and operating record.
  • Agree on baseline measures and exit criteria before implementation.

Discovery outputs by day 30

Work map

Process, user, friction, exceptions, handoffs, systems, data, and completed outcome.

Decision map

Who owns design, risk acceptance, source content, human approval, release, and operating changes.

Evaluation contract

Representative cases, expected outputs, failure categories, thresholds, review cadence, and regression owner.

Measurement baseline

Labor, queue delay, cycle time, rework, exceptions, trust/adoption, and completed-outcome evidence.

Day-30 decision

Proceed only when the intended outcome, information boundary, human authority, evaluation evidence, ownership, and recovery path are explicit enough to support a controlled pilot.

DAYS 31–45

Build the bounded workflow

  • Separate orchestration, prompt instructions, retrieval, tools, policy checks, output validation, and business logic into maintainable components.
  • Define identity, permissions, data handling, tool allowlists, limits, refusal, fallback, logging, and human checkpoints.
  • Create a reproducible test harness and versioned evaluation set.
DAYS 46–60

Stress and pilot

  • Exercise normal cases, missing context, conflicting sources, stale information, prompt injection, indirect injection, excessive permissions, tool failure, and recovery.
  • Run a controlled pilot with named users and an explicit feedback/correction path.
  • Observe completed outcomes, edits, overrides, escalations, rework, user trust, and process exceptions.
DAYS 61–75

Repair the mechanism

  • Distinguish model/prompt defects from source-data, process, permissions, interface, training, or ownership defects.
  • Tighten the envelope, retrain users where appropriate, clarify stop conditions, and rerun the test set.
  • Confirm support, monitoring, incident, and change responsibilities before broader use.
DAYS 76–90

Decide what earns reuse

  • Separate reusable prompt, retrieval, tool, evaluation, telemetry, and governance patterns from process-specific context.
  • Recommend scale, repair, hold, or stop using like-for-like baseline and pilot evidence.
  • Publish a lightweight review mechanism, ownership record, backlog of reusable patterns, and next-workflow recommendation.
