# PNC Enterprise Engineer Sr – AI: Role Intelligence

## Nominal role

PNC requisition 71280, distributed by staffing suppliers as Enterprise Engineer Sr – AI and AI Prompt Engineer. The role is Pittsburgh-based and emphasizes onsite collaboration, prompt engineering, agentic solutions, process-owner engagement, engineering practice, quality, security, and measurable business impact.

## Actual work

Turn growing enterprise AI platform availability into secure, testable, reusable internal workflows. The engineer must translate process-owner intent into technical requirements, bind prompts and agents to approved context and authority, establish evaluation evidence, preserve human decisions, and instrument adoption and operating outcomes.

## Company moment

Public reporting indicates that PNC has enhanced its enterprise AI strategy, deployed multiple generative-AI platforms including Microsoft Copilot, introduced AI-powered search, developed data-domain strategies, and continued technology modernization. PNC’s public Responsible AI materials emphasize accountability, transparency, accuracy, fairness, reliability, security, privacy, model validation, monitoring, human involvement, and business value.

This creates a source-grounded hypothesis for discovery: the organization’s next constraint is not basic access to generative AI. It is converting availability and experimentation into governed, adopted, reusable work.

## Hiring-manager context

Amy Crisanti’s public professional profile indicates a long technology career that began in software engineering. Third-party organizational information suggests a senior engineering role connected to staff-services technology; this is not treated as confirmed PNC organizational data. The interview should therefore demonstrate engineering reasoning, precise evidence, modularity, testing, risk control, process translation, and hands-on credibility rather than generic AI evangelism.

## Operating tensions

- AI speed versus bank-grade controls.
- Prompt experimentation versus maintainable engineering.
- Enterprise reuse versus process-specific context and exceptions.
- Automation versus retained human authority.
- Technical capability versus trusted adoption and measurable work.

## Decision architecture

The role should make it easier to decide:

- which internal work is suitable for AI assistance;
- what information the workflow may use;
- what tools and actions are allowed;
- what proof is required before pilot and scale;
- what decisions remain human;
- what is logged, monitored, supported, changed, or retired;
- which elements are reusable across processes;
- when to scale, repair, hold, or stop.

## Stakeholder topology

Process owners and workflow users; software and platform engineers; data and knowledge owners; information security; model risk and Responsible AI actors; quality and testing; enterprise architecture; operations and support; change and adoption partners; staffing and vendor actors.

## Success measures

- Business outcome: labor, queue delay, cycle time, rework, completion.
- Quality: task success, groundedness, completeness, refusal and edge-case behavior.
- Risk and trust: permissions, sensitive-data events, overrides, escalations, recovery.
- Adoption: use, repeat use, abandonment, user corrections, training friction.
- Economics: cost per completed outcome and avoided rework where baselines exist.
- Learning: source gaps, reusable components, regression coverage, adaptation burden.

## Change burden

Process owners must describe outcomes, exceptions, and decision boundaries. Engineering must modularize prompts, retrieval, tools, validation, and business logic. Control partners must engage early enough to shape the workflow. Users must review, correct, and escalate rather than treating output as authority. Owners must observe the operating system and maintain sources, evaluation sets, permissions, and change records.

## Candidate thesis

**Scale the work. Not just the prompt.**

An AI-assisted workflow earns scale when business intent, approved context, tool authority, evaluation evidence, human decision rights, and an operating record travel with it.

## Operating model

**AI Work Control Envelope** — six clauses assembled around one workflow and one accountable outcome. The model is neither a maturity score nor a representation of PNC’s internal governance. It is a candidate-created mechanism for a process-owner and engineering conversation.
