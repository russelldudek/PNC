## Typography evidence and decision

PNC’s public materials present a disciplined financial-services hierarchy combining strong editorial headings with highly legible sans-serif body copy. No proprietary font binaries are copied. The campaign uses Georgia for selective editorial headings and Aptos / Segoe UI / Helvetica Neue for body and interface content as broadly available substitutes. The implementation reproduces the observed character through restrained weight, compact labels, clear rules, and document density rather than claiming these are PNC’s official fonts.

## Photography, interface, and environmental cues

No generic stock photography is used. Employer recognition comes from the official mark, PNC orange and blue, disciplined white space, editorial typography, fine rules, conservative surfaces, and geometric cues derived from the PowerLink mark without tracing or modifying it.

## Role translation

The original candidate-created visual system is the **AI Work Control Envelope**. Six control clauses close around an AI-assisted workflow:

1. business intent;
2. approved context;
3. tool authority;
4. evaluation evidence;
5. human decision;
6. operating record.

The geometry is deliberately distinct from prior portfolio systems. It is not presented as a PNC product, framework, internal process, or official mark.

## Surface-by-surface use

- Candidate vision: full employer-recognition layer, signature envelope composition, PNC colors, scenario-driven control changes.
- Resume and cover letter: restrained official mark, orange rule, blue hierarchy, clean white document surface.
- Interview brief and 90-day plan: same identity and page furniture, role-specific evidence and operating model.
- AI Work Control Review: worksheet interpretation of the six clauses.

## Independent-candidate distinction

Every public route identifies the work as an independent candidate vision or candidate-created mechanism. The footer explicitly states that the work is not an official PNC publication and does not imply sponsorship or endorsement.
