Six control clauses

1 · Business intent

  • What work changes?
  • What is explicitly out of scope?
  • What is the acceptable completion boundary?

2 · Approved context

  • Which sources and data classes?
  • Who owns freshness and access?
  • What should the system do when evidence is absent or conflicting?

3 · Tool authority

  • What can the system read, write, recommend, or execute?
  • How is identity inherited?
  • What is reversible, limited, and allowlisted?

4 · Evaluation evidence

  • Representative normal and edge cases?
  • Expected outputs and failure categories?
  • Injection, permission, refusal, fallback, and regression tests?

5 · Human decision

  • Who approves, overrides, and owns exceptions?
  • Which actions always require review?
  • When must the system stop or escalate?

6 · Operating record

  • What must be logged and monitored?
  • Who owns support, incidents, and change?
  • Which outcomes, corrections, overrides, and costs matter?
