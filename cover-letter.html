Dear Ms. Crisanti,

PNC has already moved beyond debating whether generative AI belongs in the enterprise. The harder work now is translating process-owner intent into secure, testable, adopted workflows that preserve the right human decisions and produce evidence strong enough to justify scale.

That is the work I have repeatedly done at the intersection of operations, engineering, quality, and emerging technology. At Vape-Jet, I work directly across support, engineering issue flow, production, quality, Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows in a software-enabled robotics environment. Through DudeWorth, I design agentic workflow patterns connecting structured context, retrieval, human judgment, escalation, evaluation, governance, reuse, and time-to-value. Earlier roles at Amazon and Compunetics built the operating discipline behind that work: standardization, visibility, root cause, process control, technical translation, and accountable execution in high-consequence environments.

My point of view is simple: scale the work, not just the prompt. A production prompt is one component inside a bounded operating system. The workflow also needs approved context, tool authority, evaluation evidence, explicit human decision rights, fallback, telemetry, ownership, and a learning path. That makes AI useful without confusing novelty with reliability.

I created an independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/PNC/. It makes this reasoning visible through an AI Work Control Envelope, a 90-day entry plan, and a practical review mechanism for deciding when a workflow should scale, repair, hold, or stop. The work is not presented as knowledge of PNC’s undisclosed internal processes; it is a transparent demonstration of how I would enter the role and collaborate with process owners, engineers, security, quality, and governance partners.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss which unit of scale matters most to your team—users, processes, reusable engineering patterns, or platform capability—and how you want this role to change the organization’s use of AI in its first 90 days.
